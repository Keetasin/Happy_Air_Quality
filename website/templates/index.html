Welcome to Happy Air

A website that provides information about PM2.5,
including predictions of PM2.5 values in the future.

What is PM2.5?

PM2.5 stands for Particulate Matter 2.5
It refers to tiny particles or droplets in the air that are 2.5 micrometers or smaller in diameter. These particles can be made up of various substances, including dust, dirt, soot, smoke, and liquid droplets.

The causes of PM2.5

Exhaust from cars or from traffic

From information from the Pollution Control Department,Burning diesel fuel plus traffic Stuck is probably the most important reason.

Toxic air from factory chimneys Industry and power plants

burning fossil fuels or Fuel that is not environmentally friendly especially coal

open burning and in an open area

Burning of waste materials from the sector Agriculture to prepare for planting Forest burning and garbage burning

Construction

Dust generated from construction sites can contribute to elevated PM 2.5 levels in nearby areas.

levels

PM2.5 has many levels which can be divided according to the amount in the air as follows.


Good

0-12.0 ug/m3

Air quality is satisfactory and poses little or no risk.

Moderate

12.1-35.4 ug/m3

Sensitive individuals should avoid outdoor activity as they may experience respiratory symptoms.

Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups

35.5-55.4 ug/m3

General public and sensitive individuals in particular are at risk to experience irritation and respiratory problems.



Unhealthy

55.5-150.4 ug/m3

Increased likelihood of adverse effects and aggravation to the heart and lungs among general public.

Very Unhealthy

150.5-250.4 ug/m3

General public will be noticeably affected.Sensitive groups should restrict outdoor activity.

Hazardous

250.5+ ug/m3

General public at high risk of experiencing strong irritations and adverse health effects. Should avoid outdoor activities.

How does PM 2.5 affect your health?

These tiny particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream,
include: Respiratory problems, Cardiovascular effects, Irritation and inflammation, Adverse pregnancy outcomes.
